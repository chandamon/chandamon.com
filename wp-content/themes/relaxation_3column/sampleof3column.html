London New Year’s Day Parade 2006

January 2nd, 2006


In short - the annual new year parade was spectacular as usual. Over 10,000 performers from 20 countries around the world had hit the streets adorned with nearly half a million spectators (about 430,000 to be more precise) lining the streets along Westminster to Green Park. Performers included cheerleaders to marching bands, clowns, beauty pageant winners and much more. My hands were frozen stiff without gloves but my body was thankfully warm. The experience was joyous and the perfect way to start the year. It felt incredible. I cannot really describe what it feels like to see all these things happen in the place where I grew up, the place where I walk everyday - it felt magical. For all those of you who live outside the city or country - as a Londoner I would like to invite you all to attend it when the next one comes on 1st January 2007. The photo above is probably my favourite of all the pictures I took yesterday - mainly because it looks great with Big Ben as the backdrop.

Some more picks:




My personal favourite of all the performers were the American cheerleaders. They braved the cold wearing skirts and it really is impressive how much effort they make with all the jumping, dancing and chanting they do. I can imagine it is much harder than it looks. They were the star of the show and got the crowd going like no one else (I guess that is what cheerleaders are for). Most of us couldn’t help but grin as the UCA (curiously enough I don’t know what “UCA” stands for) cheerleaders danced to Elvis Presley’s “Jailhouse Rock”. Those girls also sweetly incorporated “London” in their chanting, also chanted “Happy New Year” and waved their pom-poms at everyone. For me, they stole the show.

You have to envy all the performers because there can be no doubt that it was an experience they will never forget for as long as they breathe. To perform in front of hundreds of thousands of people and across all the historical places of the city is something most of us never get to do and perhaps something most of us dream of doing. They must have had the time of their life out there.

The only regret I have is that I didn’t have a larger memory card for my digital camera and this forced me into reluctantly deleting so many good photos I could have otherwise have kept. I took about 173 photos in all but could well easily have taken 500 if I didn’t face storage restrictions. I have now made it a goal to get hold of a 1Gb memory card so that this kind of situation never arises again.

Djibril Cisse’s the guy

December 30th, 2005

It’s not hard to see why Liverpool fans like myself are into Cisse in a big way. The man clearly loves the club and is a great striker. This is in spite of the fact of non-stop ongoing rumours that he would be sold off and in spite of the fact that the manager doesn’t always get along with him. As a player it must be very unsettling to have rumours circulating all the time, especially if you spend a lot of time on the bench because if you are not in the starting line up very much then you are more likely to believe the speculation that you are no longer required at the club. Cisse admitted a few months ago that the rumours had hurt him. Yesterday his own brother spreaded those rumours by saying a deal with French club Marseille was “90% done”. Djibril today responded with “I’m in Liverpool for three more seasons and I’m not going to leave. The stuff this morning is rubbish, I’m here to stay. It hurt me because it’s my own family who talk rubbish.”

I really do hope we won’t get rid of him because clearly he loves the club and the fans love him back. It’s a mutual thing and the bond is strong. He wore a red suit on his wedding day and even dyed his hair red the day he came back from a horrific injury which saw him out of action for many months. If there is one thing that fans love more than anything else in a player, it’s sheer dedication and loyalty towards the club (except if the player is really crap, then of course the fans don’t care). Cisse shows plenty of those.

He is a great player with tremendous potential and is very passionate to help the team out. You have to love the guy for changing his hair every week as well because it makes you laugh. The above photo is of him when he scored his first ever goal. It made him so happy he did a flip. Makes me laugh every time I see it.

Merry Christmas

December 25th, 2005

Midnight mass was great - I really enjoy the Christmas chorals and all day today I have been watching it on different TV channels with my firm favourites like “O Little Town of Bethlehem“, “Hark! the Herald Angels Sing” and “Silent Night” being sang over and over again from various parts of the country. It’s amazing for how I tire of hearing them. Just a moment ago they showed Andrea Bocelli singing at the Royal Albert Hall. The very beautiful Katherine Jenkins was there too - someone I have liked for years.

Christmas is the time to reflect on all the things you care about. Christmas is about appreciating your family, your friends and all that you have. Christmas is about love.

I thoroughly enjoy the feel of the atmosphere and wish it could last longer. It is so peaceful and quiet. It’s nice to have this for a couple of days but I will be raring to go tomorrow! It’s Boxing Day and the sales will start!!!
